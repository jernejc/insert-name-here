Context

Related content

Whenever you encounter an article of content (article, post, tweet, ...) we attach to it the context it often lacks - we suggest related topics, similar articles, social comments and other contextual information. Various important entities (such as people and places) are identified within the text and connected to Wikipedia, Encyclopedia Britannica, Wolfram|Alpha or other knowledgebases. Our compiled information about the source and its reputation is also included.

With such detailed information we try to ensure you have the tools to can make an informed descision about a subject, while also taking care of our collective news diet by approaching events and subjects from diverse points of view.

Demo shows additional content attached to a tweet
Content processing

Our server cluster run a network of processing algorithms, analyzing articles from various sources and ingesting them into our expansive databases, which we maintain for optimal ease of correlating freeform text with related topics. We achieve that by using cutting edge NLP (natural language processing) algorithms for assessing structure and word importance. Once ingested, we use similarity comparisons against on important word groups so we can match articles together which prove to have the most in common.

Engagement

Report content

Users can contribute by rating an article of content to helps us asses it's validity and make sure it's written with the journalism code of ethics in mind.

Content contributions

You can suggest additional links you consider relevant to the subject. They will be analyzed through the same intake process as the rest of our content, with the addition of your reputation as an effect. We believe that will provide additional breadth of research on a given topic.

Protoype shows how a user can do further research on the topic

Reputation

Sources

The reputation for an individual source is calculated by taking into account the history of scores from the articles it's associated with. This makes the most significant factor of its credibility, consistent over time, instead of being judged based on an individual basis or entirely by short-term trends. We also take into account some of the established third-party information to assess the reputation of a given source (Web Archive, Opensources, Google News and similar).

Users

Everytime you choose to contribute to the platform by voting or reporting, we take a stock of your current reputation and account for your vote in accordance with your past voting history. We don't penalize you in any way for disagreeing with the consensus, even if your vote doesn't turn out to be relevant to the userbase at large, we still respect your preferences as much as we can - so vote as freely as you want. To have a positive affect on your reputation overall, the content voted on must be considered related by our server and have a high score.

If you would like to contribute as a journalist or another expert in your respective field, the ability to connect social accounts ( verified Twitter, verified Facebook ) would also contribute to your reputation.

Goal

[insert-name-here] is an ambitious project with a goal of bringing back the trust in the modern media. Trapped in a "clickbait" race of trying to solicit views in an oversaturated environment, the narrative is shaped not by news and events, but by emotional appeal and engineered soundbites. We believe the solution is to empower the user in making informed judgements about the content they consume, without spending hours to research every topic in detail. To achieve that, we supply you with the information needed to verify a certain topic or realize the narrative it's promoting in relation to others.

We provide a strightforward way to further research a topic at a glance, evaluate news sources, and share your contributions with each other. We keep track of content providers' reputation so they can be held accountable for their past and present work. Instead of building a separate system the goal is to be present on existing platforms so your browsing and reading habits can remain intact while offering a suite of useful information.
